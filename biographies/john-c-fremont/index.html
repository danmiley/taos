[TURNING] His third expedition (1845-1846), which passed through areas near Taos, occurred during a pivotal period of territorial transition. His mapping work contributed to U.S. understanding of the Southwest that would influence territorial acquisition and later development.

    Link to Geography/Environment¶

    Frémont's work was deeply connected to the geography and environment of northern New Mexico, including the Taos region. [HUMAN] His mapping and documentation of the region's geography, resources, and routes contributed to understanding of the Southwest's landscape and natural features. His work occurred during a period when the region's geography was being documented for U.S. expansion.

    Link to Other Figures¶

    Frémont's connections to other significant figures in Taos history include his work with guide Kit Carson, who had connections to the Taos area. [HUMAN] His collaboration with Carson during expeditions facilitated interactions with local communities and navigation of the region. His work mapping the Southwest connects him to broader exploration and territorial expansion efforts.
