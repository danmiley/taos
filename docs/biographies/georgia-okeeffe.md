## Overview

Georgia O'Keeffe stands as one of the most significant American painters associated with New Mexico, including Taos—a major artist whose paintings of the region's landscapes, light, and forms helped establish the Southwest's visual identity. `[UNUSUAL]` While O'Keeffe is more closely associated with Abiquiu and Ghost Ranch in northern New Mexico, her visits to Taos beginning in 1929 contributed to the region's artistic reputation and her own artistic development.

O'Keeffe's first visit to Taos in 1929 marked the beginning of her lifelong connection to New Mexico. `[TURNING]` Drawn by the region's dramatic landscape, distinctive light, and artistic community, she began painting New Mexico scenes that would become central to her work. Her paintings of Taos landscapes, Pueblo communities, and regional forms contributed to how Americans saw the Southwest and established her as a major landscape painter.

The paradox of O'Keeffe's story reflects broader tensions in Taos's cultural history. `[PARADOX]` She visited Taos seeking artistic inspiration and remote isolation, yet her presence, along with other major artists, contributed to the region's transformation into an art destination. This transformation, while bringing cultural recognition, also altered the very character that had initially attracted artists to Taos.
