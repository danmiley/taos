## History

### Founding `[TURNING]`

**Spanish Colonial Period**: The Questa area was part of Spanish land grants, with Hispanic settlers establishing agricultural communities in the mid-1800s.

**Territorial Period**: American acquisition brought new settlers and development, with Questa functioning as an agricultural and ranching community.

**Mining Era (1920s-2014)**: `[TURNING]` Molybdenum mining began in the 1920s, transforming Questa from agricultural community to mining town. The Molycorp molybdenum mine operated for decades, providing employment and economic stability.

**Mine Closure (2014)**: `[TURNING]` Mine closure in 2014 marked another economic transition, with the community returning to agricultural and rural economic base while facing economic challenges.

### Name Origin

**"Questa"**: The name derives from Spanish, meaning "this" or "here," reflecting the Hispanic heritage of the community.
